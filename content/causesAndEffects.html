THE PROBLEM

The Philippines is an archipelago known for the abundance of natural resources in its oceans, caves, forests, mountains, and more. However, as time progresses, more and more Philippine natural resources experience massive destruction as deforestation, mining, industrial development, and waste mismanagement prevail. These human activities severely harms the environment and, in turn, affects the lives of people that live in it.

HOW IT MANIFESTS

Consumption of Non-renewable Resources

Most of our everyday-life essentials come from non-renewable resources: fuel, oil, coal, and even some types of energy. These come from sources that, if totally consumed, will never be produced again in many, many generations. In the Philippines, fossil fuels are the main source of energy. Coal is mined and burned to be distributed to consumers.
