These are possible things that Filipinos can do about natural resource depletion.

AT HOME

  • Turn off any appliances that use up electricity when not in use.
  • Use renewable sources of energy for electricity (e.g., solar energy, wind energy, etc.).
  • Utilize what you have at home. Don't buy anything that you don't need.
  • Collect all shower water in a bucket, and use it to flush your toilet. This way, water is conserved.
  • Properly dispose of garbage. Know the procedures taken to throw away different kinds of waste.
  • Decrease the consumption of disposable items, and transition to using reusable things (e.g., safety razor, food containers, etc.).
  • Try composting food and other biodegradable material.
  • Use a cup when brushing your teeth.

OUTSIDE

  • Minimize the use of disposable utensils when eating out.
  • Walk, bike, use public transportation, or carpool to where you are going.
  • Use reusable masks to abide with COVID-19 health protocols. If not possible, dispose of surgical masks properly.
  • Take some time to appreciate nature when going outside.
  • Purchase second-hand or thrifted items as much as possible.
  • Switch to biogas for your vehicles.
